I am a master student at EPFL originally from China. I study machine learning and apply those techniques in brain sciences research. I did my bachelor thesis with Herbert Jaeger and Benn Godde on EEG classification using Echo State Neural Network at Jacobs University. In college, I aslo did an exchange program at Carnegie Mellon University. Then I worked with Moritz Grosse-Wentrup at Max Planck Tuebingen for Intelligent Systems group on transfer learning of brain-computer-interfaces. Right now I am working with Gregoire Courtine at EPFL on neuroprosthetics for spinal cord.

I strive to understand the important aspects about the brain and think about how to use brain sciences studies to create real-world applications especially in the areas of brain-computer-interfaces and human-like intelligence.

I strongly believe that in the coming decades, the fundamental research from neuroscience can be readily applicable to many much-needed applications that can change the lives of many. Therefore, I plan to launch my own company eventually to facilitate this upcoming technological revolution.
